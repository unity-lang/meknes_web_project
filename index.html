HISTORY

Founded in the 11th century by the Almoravids as a military settlement, Meknes became Morocco's capital under Sultan Moulay Ismail (1672-1727), who transformed it into an impressive city in Spanish-Moorish style. The city's name comes from the Miknasa, a Berber tribe that dominated this region in the 8th century.

Meknes was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1996 for its well-preserved historic monuments and as an exceptional example of a 17th-century Maghreb city. The city combines Islamic and European influences in its architecture and urban design.
